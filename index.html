The thesis

Four variables before you automate anything

01

Emotional intensity

How activated is the person in this moment — and does automating their interaction make a bad moment worse?

02

Decision impact

If this goes wrong, is it a one-way door? Bezos's Type 1 / Type 2 distinction, scored.

03

Relationship value

How much has this relationship cost to build, and what's actually at stake if it breaks here?

04

Revenue / reputation risk

What's the blast radius if the AI gets this specific case wrong — and is it recoverable with a retry?

Background

Operator first. Builder now.

I graduated from IIT Roorkee (Production & Industrial Engineering, 2022) and spent the years after in early-stage Indian startups — healthtech, edtech, media, fleet-tech — working across growth, product operations, CRM, and business strategy. Not writing code. Making the org actually run.

That's the part most "AI strategists" are missing: I've sat inside the workflows I now help automate. I know what breaks when you remove a human from a support queue, a sales follow-up, or a placement conversation — because I've run those queues myself.

Somewhere in the last year, building with Claude on weekends turned into a second discipline. I now spend my time where those two things meet: deciding where AI should run a process end-to-end, and building the thing that does it.

I build to validate fast — not because I want to be the engineer. At consulting or team scale, I work with a tech team. The ability to prototype and ship solo just means I can pressure-test an idea before anyone else has to spend time on it.

I write about AI automation, workflow design, and what I'm building: a few times a week on LinkedIn.
