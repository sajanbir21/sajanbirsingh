The framework

The Human Leverage Index (HLI)

Most automation decisions are made on cost and speed. The Human Leverage Index adds a third dimension: what does getting this wrong cost you?

It scores every step in a workflow across four inputs — emotional intensity of the interaction, reversibility of the decision, relationship value of the customer, and revenue risk of a bad outcome. The result is a placement decision: protect this step, review it, or automate it safely.

Workflows carry different risk profiles depending on what they're doing. A customer support interaction and a sales conversation don't fail in the same way — the framework weights the inputs accordingly. And for everything else, a calibrated default applies.

The scoring is deterministic. Same inputs, same output. You can trace every decision back to its source.

Four inputs

01

Emotional intensity

How activated is the person in this moment — angry, frustrated, neutral? Automating a high-emotion step doesn't just fail to help. It actively makes things worse.

02

Reversibility

If this goes wrong, can it be undone? Bezos's Type 1 / Type 2 distinction applied to workflow steps — irreversible decisions aren't ones AI should own outright.

03

Relationship value

How much has this relationship cost to build, and what's at stake if it breaks here? A retention-stage complaint costs more than a pre-purchase one.

04

Revenue risk

What's the blast radius if the AI gets this specific case wrong — and is it recoverable with a retry, or is the damage done?

Three verdicts

HLI ≥ 0.70

Protect

Human owns this step. The risk or relationship cost is too high to hand over.

HLI 0.40 – 0.69

Review

AI drafts, a human approves before it goes out. The middle ground most tools skip.

HLI < 0.40

Automate

Safe to hand to AI fully — low stakes, low emotional charge, low blast radius.
